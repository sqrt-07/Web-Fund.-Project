Zereshk Polo ba Morgh

A cherished Persian classic featuring delicate saffron-infused basmati rice layered with tart, sweet barberries, accompanied by tender, juicy chicken marinated and cooked in an aromatic saffron tomato sauce.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 15 minutes
  2. Cook Time: 1 hour 15 minutes
  3. Total Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  4. Servings: 4 people
  5. Difficulty: Easy
  6. Cuisine: Persian, Iranian

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken breasts (with skin and drumette attached)
  • 1 medium onion, finely sliced
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 tbsp Greek yoghurt
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for marinade)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/8 tsp ground saffron (bloomed in 2 tbsp water)
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 25 g butter (for cooking chicken)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for cooking chicken)
  • 2 cups white long-grain basmati rice (about 400g)
  • 2 tbsp salt (for soaking rice)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter
  • 1/8 tsp ground saffron (bloomed in 2 tbsp water for tahdig - crispy rice bottom)
  • 1/4 tsp ground saffron (bloomed in 2 tsp rose water and 2 tbsp water for rice garnish)
  • 5 tbsp barberries (washed)
  • 2 tbsp caster sugar (adjust sweetener to taste)
  • 1/8 tsp ground saffron (bloomed in 2 tbsp water for barberries)
  • 2 tbsp caster sugar (for barberries sauté)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken by combining sliced onion, tomato purée, Greek yoghurt, olive oil, turmeric, bloomed saffron, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Coat the chicken breasts in the marinade and refrigerate for at least 8 hours or overnight.
  2. To prepare the rice, rinse the basmati rice in cold water until water runs clear. Soak in salted water for at least 30 minutes (preferably overnight).
  3. Cook the rice by parboiling in salted boiling water until the grains are soft on the outside but still firm inside (al dente, about 3-7 minutes). Drain and rinse with cold water to stop cooking.
  4. In a saucepan, heat vegetable oil and bloomed saffron to create saffron oil. Spread a layer of rice mixed with saffron oil at the bottom to form tahdig (crispy crust). Layer the rest of the rice in a pyramid shape, reserving some saffron rice for garnish.
  5. Finish the rice by steaming on low heat with a lid wrapped in a towel for about 45 minutes to develop crispy tahdig.
  6. Separately, sauté washed barberries gently in butter with caster sugar and bloomed saffron on low heat for 30 seconds, then remove from heat.
  7. Cook the marinated chicken by first pan-frying it with olive oil and butter until golden and crispy on the skin side, then finish in the oven at 180°C (350°F) for 15-20 minutes until fully cooked and juicy.
  8. Before serving, fluff the rice and sprinkle the saffron-colored rice garnish and sautéed barberries over the top.
  9. Serve the saffron and barberry rice alongside the golden chicken breasts. Optionally accompany with fresh herbs, Salad Shirazi, Maast O’Khiar (yogurt and cucumber dip), or Persian pickles.
