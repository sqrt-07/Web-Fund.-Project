Naan Bread

Soft, pillowy Indian flatbread traditionally cooked in a tandoor oven — this easy homemade naan is perfect for scooping up your favorite curries and chutneys.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 10 minutes
  2. Rising Time: 1 hour
  3. Cook Time: 15 minutes
  4. Total Time: 1 hour 25 minutes
  5. Yield: 6-8 naan breads
  6. Difficulty: Medium
  7. Cuisine: Indian

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 ¼ cups warm water
  • ⅓ cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Butter for brushing
  • Optional: minced garlic or herbs for topping

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit until frothy, about 10 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Stir in yeast mixture, yogurt, and oil. Knead into a smooth, elastic dough.
  3. Cover and let rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  4. Divide dough into 6-8 equal pieces and roll into balls.
  5. Roll each ball into an oval shape about ¼ inch thick.
  6. Cook on a hot cast iron skillet or griddle for 2 minutes per side, until puffed and golden spots appear.
  7. Brush hot naan with melted butter and serve warm.
