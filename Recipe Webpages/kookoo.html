Kuku

A Persian-style summer frittata made with cooked kale, red onions, potatoes, and red pepper folded into eggs with aromatic spices and served with a fresh pea, mint, and feta dip — a light and delicious meal celebrating vibrant flavors and herbs.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 20 minutes
  2. Cook Time: 1 hour
  3. Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes
  4. Servings: 2-4 people
  5. Difficulty: Easy
  6. Cuisine: Persian, Iranian, Fusion

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil (plus extra to grease the tin)
  • 200 g potatoes, diced into 1 cm cubes
  • 1 medium/large red onion, finely diced
  • 1 red pepper, medium diced
  • 75 g kale (stems removed, washed, roughly chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée (dissolved in 100 ml water)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 small red chili, minced
  • 6 large free range eggs, cracked and beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C (fan) / 180°C (conventional) / Gas Mark 4. Grease and line a 20 cm diameter cake tin with baking paper and place it in the oven to heat.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the potatoes and cook until golden and slightly crispy.
  3. Add the diced red pepper and onion. Cook until softened.
  4. Add the crushed garlic, smoked paprika, and minced red chili. Stir well.
  5. Add the tomato purée diluted in water and stir to combine.
  6. Add the kale and cook until wilted and the mixture has very little or no liquid left. Turn off the heat and let it cool for 10 minutes.
  7. In a large bowl, beat the eggs, then add the cooled vegetable mixture. Season with salt and pepper and mix thoroughly.
  8. Remove the hot cake tin from the oven and pour the mixture in. Bake in the oven for 30 to 40 minutes or until a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Allow to cool slightly before slicing. Serve warm or cold, optionally accompanied by fresh pea, mint, and feta dip, flatbreads, or antipasti.
