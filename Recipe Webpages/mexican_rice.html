Best Mexican Rice

A fluffy, flavor-packed Mexican rice cooked with sautéed carrots, bell peppers, onions, and garlic, toasted with tomato paste and simmered in a rich, savory tomato broth with cumin and oregano, topped with fresh cilantro.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 10 minutes
  2. Cook Time: 25 minutes
  3. Total Time: 35 minutes
  4. Servings: 4-6
  5. Difficulty: Easy
  6. Cuisine: Mexican

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 carrots, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 small green bell pepper, seeds and ribs removed, finely chopped
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups long-grain rice, rinsed and drained
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 (14-ounce) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add carrots, bell pepper, onion, and garlic. Cook, stirring frequently, until onions are softened and translucent, about 7 minutes.
  2. Add the rice and tomato paste, stirring often; cook until the rice smells nutty and the paste darkens slightly, about 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth, diced tomatoes, and tomato sauce. Add cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine, scraping up browned bits from the pan bottom.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then cover and reduce heat to low. Simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and the liquid is fully absorbed, about 17 minutes.
  5. Fluff the rice with a fork, remove from heat, and top with fresh cilantro before serving.
