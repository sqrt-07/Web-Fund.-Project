Char Siu Bao

Soft, fluffy steamed buns filled with sweet and savory Chinese BBQ pork — a beloved dim sum classic enjoyed worldwide.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 2 hours (includes dough rising)
  2. Cook Time: 15 minutes steaming
  3. Total Time: About 2 hours 30 minutes
  4. Servings: 12 buns
  5. Difficulty: Medium
  6. Cuisine: Chinese, Dim Sum

Ingredients

  • For the dough:
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • For the filling:
  • 1 cup char siu (Chinese BBQ pork), diced
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add yeast mixture and vegetable oil. Knead until smooth and elastic (about 8-10 minutes).
  3. Cover dough with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm place for about 1 hour until doubled in size.
  4. Meanwhile, prepare the filling: heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  5. Add diced char siu, oyster sauce, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir well.
  6. Add dissolved cornstarch and cook until mixture thickens. Remove from heat and let cool.
  7. Divide dough into 12 equal pieces. Roll each into a circle and place 1-2 tablespoons filling in the center.
  8. Gather edges and pinch to seal. Place on parchment squares and let rest for 20 minutes.
  9. Steam buns over boiling water for 12-15 minutes until puffed and cooked through.
  10. Serve hot as a delightful dim sum treat.
