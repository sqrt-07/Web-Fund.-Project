Akbarjoojeh

A whole young chicken, marinated to tender perfection and pan-fried in rich butter until golden and crispy, served alongside fragrant saffron rice and a vibrant pomegranate reduction — a celebrated northern Persian classic bursting with flavor.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 15 minutes
  2. Marinating Time: 12-24 hours
  3. Cook Time: 30-40 minutes
  4. Total Time: Approx. 13-25 hours (including marinating)
  5. Servings: 4-6 people
  6. Difficulty: Medium
  7. Cuisine: Persian - Mazandaran

Ingredients

  • 1 whole young chicken (under 1 kg), cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, peeled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup walnut, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tablespoons pomegranate paste (morabba anar)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Cooking oil or butter, for frying

Instructions

  1. Skin and wash the whole chicken, then cut it into parts (you can leave wings whole or cut).
  2. In a large bowl, combine chopped onion, garlic, lemon juice, pomegranate paste, turmeric, black pepper, and salt.
  3. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, mix thoroughly to coat, and refrigerate for 12 to 24 hours for best flavor.
  4. Remove chicken from marinade 15 minutes before cooking.
  5. Heat oil or butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces (remove onion bits if present) and fry until golden on all sides.
  6. Lower the heat, cover the pot, and cook chicken for 20-30 minutes, turning occasionally to avoid burning.
  7. In the last 2-3 minutes of cooking, add salt and melt butter in the pan; stir-fry chicken with butter to enrich flavor.
  8. Serve the chicken hot, traditionally alongside saffron rice and pomegranate sauce for dipping.
