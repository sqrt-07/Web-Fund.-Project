Texas-Style Beef Brisket

Thick slices of tender, slow-cooked beef brisket kissed with smoky, sweet, and tangy barbecue sauce — a no-smoke Texas classic you can make in your slow cooker.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: Overnight marinating
  2. Cook Time: 6 to 8 hours
  3. Total Time: Approx. 7 to 9 hours
  4. Servings: 8-10 people
  5. Difficulty: Medium
  6. Cuisine: Texan, American BBQ

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon celery salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon liquid smoke (optional)
  • 1 fresh beef brisket (6 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Barbecue Sauce:
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1/4 cup cider vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground mustard

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl or shallow dish, combine Worcestershire sauce, chili powder, minced garlic, celery salt, black pepper, and liquid smoke if using.
  2. Cut the brisket in half, add to the marinade, and turn to coat. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  3. Transfer the brisket to a 5- or 6-quart slow cooker. Add beef broth and bay leaves. Cover and cook on low for 6 to 8 hours or until meat is tender.
  4. For the barbecue sauce: In a small saucepan, sauté chopped onion in canola oil until tender. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
  5. Stir in ketchup, molasses, cider vinegar, chili powder, and ground mustard. Heat through until sauce is warm and aromatic.
  6. Remove brisket from slow cooker and discard bay leaves. Place 1 cup of the cooking juices in a measuring cup and skim off fat. Discard remaining juices.
  7. Add the reserved cooking juices to the barbecue sauce.
  8. Return the brisket to the slow cooker and top with the barbecue sauce. Cover and cook on high for 30 minutes to allow flavors to blend.
  9. Thinly slice the brisket against the grain and serve warm with the sauce.
