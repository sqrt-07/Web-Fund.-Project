Sartu di Riso

A classic Neapolitan rice timbale baked with a savory filling of meatballs, peas, cheese, and tomato sauce — a festive and elegant Italian main dish.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  2. Cook Time: 1 hour
  3. Total Time: 2 hours 30 minutes
  4. Servings: 6-8
  5. Difficulty: Medium
  6. Cuisine: Italian, Neapolitan

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup cooked peas
  • 12-15 small meatballs (cooked)
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, cubed
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook Arborio rice in chicken broth until creamy and tender. Stir in butter and set aside to cool.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine cooked rice with eggs, half of the Parmesan, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grease a ring mold or deep baking pan. Press half the rice mixture into the bottom and sides to form a shell.
  4. Layer cooked meatballs, peas, mozzarella, and some tomato sauce inside the rice shell.
  5. Top with remaining rice mixture, pressing to seal.
  6. Pour tomato sauce over the top and sprinkle remaining Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 1 hour until set and golden.
  8. Let cool slightly, then unmold carefully. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve warm.
