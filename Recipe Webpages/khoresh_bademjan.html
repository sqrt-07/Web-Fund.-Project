Khoresh Bademjan (Persian Eggplant Stew with Beef)

A classic Persian stew combining tender chunks of beef with baked eggplants simmered in a rich, tangy tomato sauce, accented with dried lime and saffron — a beloved comfort food served over steamed rice.

Recipe Info

  1. Prep Time: 20 minutes
  2. Cook Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  3. Total Time: 1 hour 50 minutes
  4. Servings: 4-6 people
  5. Difficulty: Medium
  6. Cuisine: Persian

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 500 g lamb or beef, cut into bite-sized pieces (leg or shoulder preferred)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 small clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons concentrated tomato paste
  • 200 g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 250 ml water
  • 1 tablespoon liquid saffron (saffron soaked in hot water)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 dried lime (limoo amani) or substitute: juice of 1 lime or 1 teaspoon sumac
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 7-10 medium Indian or Italian eggplants, peeled and baked or grilled

Instructions

  1. In a medium stockpot, combine the cubed meat, water, turmeric, chopped onion, and black pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer covered for 1 to 1½ hours until the meat is tender. Discard the onion after cooking.
  2. While the meat cooks, bake or grill the peeled and sliced eggplants until soft and slightly caramelized.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil and fry the thinly sliced onions until golden brown.
  4. Add the tomato paste to the fried onions and sauté over medium-low heat for 5 minutes until aromatic.
  5. Add the broth from the cooked meat and salt to the tomato-onion mixture, bring to a boil to make the sauce.
  6. Add the cooked meat, baked eggplants, dried lime (or substitute), and saffron liquid to the skillet. Bring to a boil again, then reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour until the sauce thickens and the flavors meld.
  7. Adjust salt and pepper to taste before serving.
  8. Serve hot over steamed Persian white rice with tahdig if desired.
